## The "Fear of Missing Out"
You remember Team Fortress 2. A timeless classic. Vibrant. Fast-paced. But don't let its age fool you. To truly *experience* TF2—flawless visuals, crisp 144+ FPS in chaotic 32-player servers, zero stutter—your rig needs to perform. Anything less means you're missing out on the game's true glory.

Running TF2 isn't about just launching it. It's about dominating. An ancient CPU from pre-2020? A weak integrated GPU? You'll be dropping frames when the action heats up. The Source engine, while old, can still hammer your hardware with particle effects, custom content, and high player counts. Don't let your gear hold you back. SpecMatch has done the deep dive.

## Why You Need This Power
*   **Source Engine Demands:** It's old, but surprisingly CPU-bound in competitive scenarios. High player counts and custom servers push even modern processors.
*   **Particle Effects Chaos:** Explosions, flame throwers, Medic beams—these generate a massive number of particles. Maxed out, they can tank your FPS if your GPU isn't ready.
*   **Competitive FPS:** You want 144+ FPS *consistently*. Not just "running the game." Every frame matters for hit registration and quick reflexes. Don't settle.
